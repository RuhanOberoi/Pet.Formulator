🚀 Getting Started

New to PetForm Pro? Start here.

What is PetForm Pro?

PetForm Pro is a pet food formulation tool that helps you build, analyse, and verify recipes for dogs and cats. It checks your formulas against AAFCO and FEDIAF nutritional standards, calculates ingredient costs, and gives you clear feedback on what's deficient or excessive.

It's designed for pet food formulators, manufacturers, veterinary nutritionists, and serious home cooks who want to make sure their recipes meet real nutritional requirements.

I just signed up — what should I do first?

When you sign up, you'll see a sample formula already loaded in the Builder — "Adult Dog Maintenance — Chicken & Rice". This shows you what a complete recipe looks like.

Try these to get familiar with the tool:

  • Click on an ingredient amount in the recipe table to edit it — watch the totals change
  • Click the Compliance tab to see how the recipe stacks up against AAFCO standards
  • Click Ingredients tab to browse the catalog of 50+ built-in ingredients
  • When you're ready, click "Start Fresh" on the welcome banner to clear the sample and build your own
How do I build a recipe from scratch?
  1. Go to the Builder tab
  2. Set the species (Dog/Cat) and life stage (Adult, Puppy/Kitten, Senior, etc.) at the top
  3. Choose a food format (Dry/Kibble, Wet/Canned, Raw, etc.) — this affects the moisture target
  4. From the ingredient library on the left, click + Add next to any ingredient
  5. Adjust amounts in the recipe table
  6. Aim for a 1000g (1kg) total — the tool will tell you when you're close
  7. Click the Compliance tab to verify nutritional adequacy
My account works on multiple devices — how?

Yes. When you sign up, your account is stored securely in the cloud (via Supabase). Your formulas, custom ingredients, and saved settings sync automatically across any device — phone, tablet, laptop, office computer.

Just sign in with the same email and password from any browser.

🛠 Using the Tool

Builder, Compliance, Ingredients, Custom Standards.

What does "As-Fed" vs "Dry Matter" mean?

Two ways to express nutrient values:

  • As-Fed (AsF): Nutrient amounts in the food as it sits in the bowl, INCLUDING moisture. This is what you actually feed.
  • Dry Matter (DM): Nutrient amounts AFTER removing all moisture. This lets you compare a wet food (75% moisture) to a kibble (10% moisture) on equal footing.

AAFCO and FEDIAF requirements are typically stated on a Dry Matter basis. PetForm Pro defaults to As-Fed (matching commercial workflow), but you can toggle to DM in the Compliance tab.

How do I add a custom ingredient?
  1. Go to the Ingredients tab
  2. Click + Add Custom in the top right
  3. Fill in the name, category, cost per kg, and key nutrient values
  4. You can also import from the USDA Database (click "🔍 USDA Database") or upload a CSV/JSON file

Custom ingredients sync to the cloud and appear alongside built-in ingredients in the Builder.

Can I edit the built-in ingredient values?

Yes. Click ✎ Edit on any built-in ingredient. Your edits create a "MODIFIED" override that's saved to your account. The original built-in values stay intact for other users.

To revert to the original: click the ↺ reset button on a modified ingredient.

What is the Auto-Fix Recipe feature?

Click the ✨ Fix Recipe button in the Builder toolbar. It analyses your current recipe and suggests adjustments to ingredient amounts to better meet AAFCO standards (e.g., "increase fish oil to boost omega-3", "add limestone to fix calcium deficiency").

This is a heuristic helper — always review the suggestions and use your professional judgement before applying them.

How do I save my recipe?

Click the 💾 Save button in the Builder toolbar. Give your formula a name and optional notes. It's saved to your cloud library.

To load a saved formula, go to the Library tab and click on any saved formula.

Free tier: 3 saved formulas. Pro tier: Unlimited saves.
What are Custom Standards?

Custom Standards let you define your own nutrient targets that go beyond AAFCO/FEDIAF — useful for:

  • Veterinary therapeutic diets (e.g., kidney support, weight loss)
  • Breed-specific requirements
  • Internal quality standards for a brand
  • Research formulations

Click + New Standard in the Builder, prefill from AAFCO or FEDIAF as a starting point, then adjust min/max values for any nutrient.

How do I export my formula?

From the Library tab or after building a formula:

  • PDF Report: Click "Export PDF" — generates a printable summary
  • CSV/Excel: Click "Export CSV" — gets all ingredients and nutrient values as a spreadsheet
  • JSON: For backup or sharing with another PetForm Pro user

📋 AAFCO & FEDIAF Basics

What these standards mean and how PetForm Pro uses them.

What is AAFCO?

AAFCO (Association of American Feed Control Officials) publishes nutritional adequacy standards for pet foods sold in the United States. Their nutrient profiles list minimum (and sometimes maximum) levels of protein, fat, vitamins, minerals, and amino acids needed for dogs and cats at different life stages.

PetForm Pro uses AAFCO 2023 nutrient profiles by default.

What is FEDIAF?

FEDIAF (European Pet Food Industry Federation) is the European equivalent of AAFCO. Their nutritional guidelines apply to pet foods sold in the EU and UK. Some nutrient minimums and maximums differ from AAFCO.

You can switch between AAFCO and FEDIAF in the Builder's standards selector.

Does meeting AAFCO mean my recipe is "safe"?
⚠ Important: Meeting AAFCO standards is necessary but NOT sufficient for individual pet health.

AAFCO standards are minimums for healthy adult dogs and cats. They do NOT account for:

  • Individual health conditions (kidney disease, diabetes, allergies, etc.)
  • Breed-specific predispositions
  • Bioavailability of nutrients from specific ingredients
  • Long-term feeding effects

Always consult a veterinarian or veterinary nutritionist before feeding any home-formulated diet, especially to pets with existing health conditions, growing puppies/kittens, or pregnant/lactating animals.

Why does my recipe show some nutrients as deficient?

The Compliance tab compares your recipe against AAFCO/FEDIAF requirements. If a nutrient falls below the minimum, you'll see a red warning with the gap (e.g., "Calcium: 0.85% DM, needs ≥1.0% DM").

To fix deficiencies:

  • Look at the top contributor hint shown next to each warning — it suggests which ingredient most affects that nutrient
  • Add more of that ingredient, or add a supplement (limestone for calcium, fish oil for DHA, etc.)
  • Use the ✨ Fix Recipe button for automated suggestions

✨ AI Features

AI Builder, AI Analyser, and usage limits.

What does the AI Builder do?

Describe what you want in plain English (e.g., "build a grain-free senior dog food using salmon and sweet potato") and the AI generates a complete formula meeting AAFCO standards. You can refine it, ask for changes, or apply it directly to the Builder.

AI Builder is available on Pro and Pro+ plans.

What does the AI Analyser do?

Looks at your current recipe and explains what's working, what's not, and how to improve it. Different from the Auto-Fix button — the Analyser gives you context and reasoning, not just numerical suggestions.

Useful when you want to understand WHY a nutrient is off, not just FIX it.

How many AI calls do I get per month?
  • Free: 0 AI calls
  • Pro ($14/mo): 20 AI calls/month
  • Pro+ ($29/mo): 100 AI calls/month

Counters reset on the 1st of each calendar month.

If you upgrade mid-month, you keep your used count but get the higher limit. For example, if you've used 5 Pro calls (5/20) and upgrade to Pro+, you'll see 5/100.

What's the difference between AI Builder and AI Analyser?

AI Builder: Creates a NEW recipe from your description.

AI Analyser: Reviews your EXISTING recipe and gives feedback.

Both count toward your monthly AI quota.

Are AI suggestions safe to feed my pet?
⚠ Important: AI outputs are starting points, not veterinary prescriptions.

The AI uses Anthropic's Claude model and follows AAFCO/FEDIAF guidelines, but:

  • It cannot account for your pet's specific health needs
  • It can occasionally make mistakes in nutrient calculations
  • It doesn't replace professional veterinary advice

Always verify AI-generated formulas with the Compliance tab AND consult a vet before feeding.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Common issues and how to fix them.

My save failed — where did my formula go?

If a cloud save fails, your formula is automatically kept in your browser's local storage. You won't lose your work.

Try refreshing the page — the formula should still be there. Then try saving again.

If saves keep failing, check:

  • Are you signed in? (Sometimes sessions time out — sign out and back in)
  • Is your internet stable?
  • Are you on the free tier with 3 formulas already saved? (Free tier has a 3-formula limit)
I paid for Pro+ but my AI counter still shows 20 calls

This usually resolves within 30 seconds of payment. If not:

  1. Sign out completely
  2. Sign back in
  3. Your AI counter should now show "X / 100"

If it still shows 20, please contact support with your payment receipt.

The AI says "quota exceeded" but I haven't used many calls

Check the counter at the top of the app — it shows your used calls / limit for this month.

The counter resets on the 1st of each calendar month, NOT 30 days from your subscription date.

If the counter looks wrong, sign out and back in to refresh.

The Builder/Ingredients tab looks broken or weird

Try a hard refresh: Ctrl+Shift+R (Windows/Linux) or Cmd+Shift+R (Mac). This clears the browser cache and reloads the latest version.

If that doesn't help, try opening the app in incognito/private mode to rule out browser extensions or stale cache.

I forgot my password

On the sign-in page, click "Forgot password?" and enter your email. You'll receive a password reset link.

If the email doesn't arrive, check your spam folder.

I can't find my saved formulas

Saved formulas appear in the Library tab. If they're missing:

  • Make sure you're signed in with the same email you used to save them
  • Refresh the page
  • If you saved them while offline, they may still be in your browser only — sign in and they should sync
